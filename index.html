Zoom meeting 1 — heavy rain & lab follow-ups Dec 17, 2025

Effects of heavy rain on pond

  • Atmospheric river / Pineapple Express-type storms possible for the Bay Area; check Pleasant Hill, CA forecast.
  • Small, shallow pond → responds quickly to rain; temperature swings may affect metabolism.
  • Possible new organisms washed in after heavy rain.

pH & KH

  • Rainwater often slightly acidic (~5.0–5.5); can stress buffering.
  • KH around 25 mg/L noted as low; compare to typical stability targets (often cited ~70–150 mg/L for small ponds—verify with your sources).
  • Low buffer → pH can swing; sudden drops can stress organisms.

Green water & oxygen

  • Rain carries nutrients → may fuel algae (“green water”). Check clarity over break.
  • Rain may stir sediment → more organic matter for aerobic bacteria → possible DO dip (test after break).
Hypotheses—confirm with post-storm measurements.

Improving future experiments

  • TS (evaporating dish): add liquid as it evaporates; need larger sample; track TSS & TDS.
  • KH: unclear trend; ~25 at all ponds in notes—decide if retesting is needed.
  • Continue: nitrite, chlorine, KH, TS, TDS, TSS, DO2.

Potential research topics

  • Manmade vs natural pond; cold effects; animals; plants; biodiversity; life types; native life.
  • TS = TSS + TDS; literature on TDS/TSS measurement methods.
  • Rain effects; pond cleanup without disturbing ecosystem; tools; algae (good vs bad; quant methods); plants; cattails; ecosystem type; size/depth vs original construction.

Discussion covered chlorine, pH (higher on a rainy collection day), copper and lead (0—no need to continue), carbonate (0 all ponds), ammonium nitrogen (0), sodium chloride, nitrate—stop routine testing when consistently zero.

  • Temperature: fairly consistent.
  • Alkalinity: large spread—e.g. 80, 80, 80 vs pond three 40 (from recent table). Ask whether bicarbonate is added upstream and trickles through ponds 0→1→2 vs 3.
  • Focus: alkalinity, pH, hardness—compare to normal ranges from homework/sources; calibrate pH strips.
  • Retest if recent vs previous tables disagree sharply; rainy-day collection as a control idea.
  • Chlorine: if a reading looked implausible (e.g. pond 2), retest—very high free chlorine would imply an unhealthy pond; may be procedural (strip left in too long).
  • Bubbles / murkiness (pond 2 photos): many causes—overfeeding, organic debris, seasonal leaves (phosphate?), flow between ponds.
  • Campus context: blue dye used for algae; consider phosphate remover recommendation; good filtration helps algae control.
  • Questions for James Buchanan (possible next term): how much algae is necessary? Is the current system adequate?

Meeting 2 — pond factors Oct 17, 2025

Ammonia (Isaac)

  • Fertilizers, cleaners; metabolic waste; precursor to other nitrogen compounds.
  • Speciation depends on pH/temp; measure TAN; good feeding practices reduce ammonia.

CaCO3 (Natalie)

  • Lime/alkalinity; stabilizes pH; soil tests to plan additions; rain lowers alkalinity—retest with soil/alkalinity tests.

pH (Sam)

  • Log scale; typical range often cited ~6.5–9.0; managed ponds may see wider swings—optimal band in notes ~6.0–9.0.
  • Stress, reproduction, growth, disease, diversity; pollutant speciation (e.g. copper, ammonia).
  • Low pH < 6 / high pH > 9; links to respiration & photosynthesis.

Alkalinity

  • Buffering capacity; CO2–carbonate system; low alkalinity ↔ lower pH tendency, high alkalinity ↔ higher—qualitative framing from notes.

Dissolved O2 (Rita)

  • Optimal ~6–7 mg/L (depends on pond goals); season/weather; supersaturation risks; Secchi related to clarity.

Temperature (Aaron)

  • ~68–74 °F; >~85 °F can worsen O2 stress; stratification, algae, nutrients.
  • Seasonal care: ice holes/de-icer, spring fountain shock, summer aeration, fall feeding reduction, etc.

Chlorine (Calista)

  • Target 0 ppm; fish tolerate ~0.01 ppm order of magnitude per notes—very low tolerance.

TSS (Toby)

  • Particles > 2 µm; turbidity; high TSS can cut light, affect DO; typical “good” target in notes < 30 mg/L.

Secchi disks (Malahim / Brenda)

  • Clarity vs algae; need pond depth context for interpretation.

Experiments to plan

  • pH strips vs probe; frequency; trends; variability.
  • Temperature logging across depth, shade, season, time of day.
  • Nitrogen species (ammonia → nitrite → nitrate).
  • Depth mapping; blueprint.
  • TSS / turbidity / DIY vacuum filter.

Priority

Depth first (may be negligible if shallow)—decide measurement method.
